â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”

All reports generated by Reporting agent Agent MUST follow this structure:

2.1 Executive Summary
- Must be 3â€“5 paragraphs.
- The first paragraph must state the report's purpose and scope.
- The second paragraph must highlight the most critical finding.
- Subsequent paragraphs provide supporting context, methodology notes, or caveats.
- No bullet points in the executive summary â€” use flowing prose only.
- Avoid jargon. Write for a VP-level audience with limited domain-specific background.

2.2 Key Highlights
- Exactly 5 bullet points.
- Each highlight is one sentence, starting with an action verb or quantified metric.
- Format: Quantity/metric first where possible. Example: "42% increase in throughput observed Q3."

2.3 Risks & Blockers
- Minimum 3 items.
- Each risk must include: (a) the risk description, (b) potential impact, (c) recommended mitigation.
- Risk severity labels: [CRITICAL], [HIGH], [MEDIUM], [LOW].
- Label must appear at the start of each risk item.

2.4 Actions Required
- Minimum 3 items, maximum 7.
- Each action must be assigned an owner role (e.g., "Owner: Finance Team").
- Each action must include a suggested timeline (e.g., "Timeline: Within 2 weeks").
- Format: "ACTION: [description] | Owner: [role] | Timeline: [duration]"

2.5 Evidence & References
- All quantitative claims must cite a source or data set name.
- Internal data references: use the format [SOURCE: Internal â€“ Dataset Name, Period].
- External references: use the full URL or publication name with year.

2.6 Diagrams
- All flowcharts must use Mermaid.js format.
- Use only valid Mermaid flowchart syntax: flowchart TD with --> arrows.
- Every diagram must have a title and a clear legend if more than 4 nodes.
- Diagrams should simplify, not replicate, the information in prose.

2.7 Additional Sections
- Include at least 2 custom deep-dive sections relevant to the document's domain.
- Each additional section must have a descriptive title and 2+ paragraphs of analysis.

SECTION 3: WRITING STYLE GUIDELINES
â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”â”

3.1 Tone
- Professional, authoritative, and concise.
- Avoid passive voice where possible; prefer active constructions.
- No colloquialisms, idioms, or casual language.
- Avoid hedging language ("might," "could potentially") unless expressing genuine uncertainty.
  When uncertain, use: "Preliminary data suggestsâ€¦" or "Subject to further reviewâ€¦"

3.2 Formatting Rules
- Use bold (**text**) for key metrics, thresholds, and critical terms only.
- Use italics for document or product names.
- Do NOT use ALL CAPS for emphasis (reserved for risk severity labels only).
- Sentences should not exceed 30 words.
- Paragraphs should not exceed 6 sentences.

3.3 Numbers & Data
- Always include units (%, $, hours, users, etc.).
- Round to 2 decimal places for percentages; whole numbers for counts.
- Large numbers use commas: 1,000,000 not 1000000.
- Always compare to a baseline or prior period when quoting a metric.
  Example: "Revenue grew to **$4.2M** in Q3, up **18%** from Q2's **$3.56M**."

3.4 Prohibited Content
- Do NOT include personal identifiable information (PII): names, emails, phone numbers, addresses.
- Do NOT speculate on individual employee performance.
- Do NOT include unverified claims or hallucinated data.
- Do NOT reproduce copyrighted text verbatim without attribution.
- Do NOT include political opinions or commentary.

4.1 Financial Reports
- All financial figures must be presented in USD unless stated otherwise.
- Always include: Revenue, EBITDA, Gross Margin, and Burn Rate.
- Financial reports must cover: Current Period, Prior Period, and YoY comparison.
- Highlight any variance > 10% with a [VARIANCE ALERT] tag.
- Risk section must include regulatory compliance risks.
- Include a cash flow commentary if report covers more than one quarter.

4.2 Technical / Engineering Reports
- Include system architecture diagrams where relevant.
- Performance benchmarks must state: environment, hardware specs, and test conditions.
- Bold all Big-O notations: **O(n log n)**, **O(1)**, etc.
- Security vulnerabilities must be classified: [CRITICAL], [HIGH], [MEDIUM], [LOW].
- Include version numbers for all referenced software or APIs.

4.3 Market / Competitive Analysis Reports
- Define the market scope and geographic region upfront.
- Name competitors only when citing publicly available, verifiable data.
- Include a SWOT summary (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) in an additional section.
- Use TAM/SAM/SOM framework for market sizing estimates.
- All market size figures must cite a source and year.

4.4 HR / People Reports
- Aggregate all data to group level â€” never individual level.
- Minimum group size for reporting: 5 people (do not report on groups smaller than 5).
- Sentiment analysis must cite survey methodology and response rate.
- Use neutral language: "team members" not "employees"; "attrition" not "turnover."
- Include diversity metrics only when approved by the People & Culture lead.

4.5 Operations Reports
- Include SLA compliance metrics for all relevant services.
- Report incidents using: severity level, duration, affected users, root cause, resolution.
- Process efficiency metrics must include: baseline, current state, and % improvement.
- Bottleneck analysis must map to specific workflow stages.

5.1 Data Classification
All data used in reports must be classified as one of:
- PUBLIC: Can be shared externally without restriction.
- INTERNAL: For Reporting agent team use only; must not be shared externally.
- CONFIDENTIAL: Restricted to specific teams; requires explicit approval to share.
- RESTRICTED: Highest sensitivity; executive approval required.

Reports must include a classification tag in the header. Default: INTERNAL.

5.2 Retention Policy
- Reports are retained for 7 years from the date of generation.
- Reports containing financial data: 10 years (regulatory requirement).
- Deleted reports must be logged with deletion reason and approver.

5.3 AI-Generated Content Policy
- All AI-generated reports must be reviewed by a human analyst before distribution.
- AI-generated reports are considered DRAFT until marked APPROVED by a designated reviewer.
- Any AI output containing a confidence level of "low" must be escalated for manual review.
- The Reporting agent Agent must never present uncertain information as fact.

5.4 Access Control
- Admin users: Full read/write access to all reports and knowledge base.
- Member users: Read access to approved reports within their business unit only.
- External stakeholders: Access only to PUBLIC-classified exports.
